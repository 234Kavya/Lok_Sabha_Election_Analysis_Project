State-wise Analysis: A Comprehensive Review

The political landscape of India is diverse, with each state uniquely contributing to electoral outcomes. This analysis covers voter turnout, seat count changes, and vote share distribution.

Voter Turnout by State

Top States: Uttar Pradesh (240), Maharashtra (200), West Bengal (200), Bihar (200)
Smaller Turnout: Ladakh, Sikkim, Pondicherry
Larger states show higher voter turnout due to their population and engagement levels.

Count of States and Parties Over the Years

-2004: 39 parties, 35 states
2019: 36 parties, 36 states
2024: 42 parties, 37 states
There has been a decline in party count but stable state count, suggesting political consolidation.

Vote Share Distribution

Top States: Uttar Pradesh (13.9%), West Bengal (9.51%), Maharashtra (8.8%), Tamil Nadu (7.1%)
Populous states have significant electoral influence.

Changes in Seat Count

-Top Changes: Uttar Pradesh (400), Maharashtra (240), West Bengal (200)
Shifts in seat counts indicate changing political representation.


Conclusion The analysis highlights the electoral importance of larger states like Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and West Bengal, emphasizing their crucial roles in shaping India's political landscape.
